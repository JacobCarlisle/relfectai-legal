Overview

ReflectAI helps you record voice reflections, generate transcripts, and surface AI-generated insights about your mood and patterns.

If you have questions about this policy, contact us at jacobrcarlisle@gmail.com or visit https://jacobcarlisle.github.io/relfectai-legal/support.html.

Information We Collect

  • Account information, such as your email address and authentication identifiers.
  • Voice recordings you choose to create in the app.
  • Transcripts generated from your recordings.
  • Journal insights and mood-related summaries generated from your content.
  • Subscription and purchase entitlement information provided by Apple.
  • Basic diagnostic and usage records necessary to operate rate limiting, security, and service reliability.

How We Use Information

  • Create and manage your account.
  • Transcribe your voice recordings.
  • Generate AI-powered insights and summaries.
  • Save and sync your journal history.
  • Restore and verify subscription access.
  • Protect the service from abuse, fraud, and unauthorized access.
  • Respond to support requests and improve the app.

Storage and Retention

Your journal entries, transcripts, and related metadata may be stored in your account so you can access prior reflections.

  • You can delete specific entries inside the app.
  • You can delete your account from Settings.
  • We may retain limited records when needed for security, legal, or billing purposes.

Account Deletion

You can delete your account inside the app from Settings.

Deleting your account removes your ReflectAI account and associated cloud journal data, subject to any limited retention required by law or security needs.

Deleting your account does not automatically cancel an App Store subscription. Subscription billing remains managed by Apple.

Sharing of Information

We do not sell your personal information.

  • Infrastructure, authentication, database, and AI processing providers that help operate ReflectAI.
  • Apple for in-app purchase and subscription management.
  • Law enforcement or regulators when required by applicable law.
